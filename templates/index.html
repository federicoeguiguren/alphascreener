How Stocks Are Scored

Each stock receives four category grades (1–10), combined into an Overall Grade using the weights below.

💰

Value

30% weight

Measures how cheaply a stock trades relative to its fundamentals. Cheap stocks tend to outperform over long horizons.

  • Book-to-Market — book value ÷ price
  • Earnings Yield — EPS ÷ price (inverse P/E)
⚙️

Quality

35% weight

Measures business strength — profitability, balance-sheet health, and earnings reliability.

  • Gross-Profit Ratio — gross profit ÷ assets
  • ROA — return on assets
  • Current Ratio — short-term liquidity
  • Cash-Flow Quality — operating cash ÷ net income
  • Piotroski F-Score — 9-point financial health check
  • Earnings Quality — low accruals = high quality
🚀

Momentum

20% weight

Stocks that have risen tend to keep rising. Momentum captures this persistence in returns.

  • 12-Month Return — trailing year price gain
  • 1-Month Return — recent short-term trend
🛡️

Safety

15% weight

Low-risk stocks (low volatility, low debt) tend to outperform on a risk-adjusted basis.

  • Volatility — annualised daily return std dev
  • Beta — market sensitivity
  • Leverage — total debt ÷ equity
  • Skewness — return distribution shape

Methodology

1

Fetch Universe

All S&P 500 constituents are pulled weekly from Wikipedia.

2

Compute Signals

14 raw financial signals are calculated per stock using 5-year historical data.

3

Rank & Grade

Each signal is ranked within the S&P 500 universe and converted to a 1–10 grade.

4

Combine

Category grades are weighted and averaged into one Overall Grade per stock.
